# How to Read This Book {.unnumbered}

This book has five doors, and it was built so that you need only one of
them.

If you build machine translation or work with language models, enter
through the question of evaluation: how would you ever know a translation
of a philosophical text is right? The answer here is a working system,
and its architecture transfers to any domain where a model proposes and
a checker must dispose.

If you care about theorem provers, enter through the domain: a
formalization target that is not mathematics, with a small decidable
kernel and an exotic, serious source of design ideas in Navya-Nyāya.

If you love Sanskrit literature, enter through the text: the complete
first book of the Vākyapadīyam, in Devanagari and transliteration, with
translation and full commentary, verse by verse.

If you study vyākaraṇa, enter through the tradition: Pāṇini to
Bhartṛhari taken seriously as formal systems, and carried forward.

If commentary itself is your subject, enter through the vṛtti: this
whole project treats the commentary as the spine of meaning and makes
every interpretive ruling explicit, cited, and checkable.

## Reading paths {.unnumbered}

The chapters form one story and reward reading in order, but each path
below is self-sufficient. Chapter 1 is for everyone; it states the
problem the whole book answers.

**The philosophy path.** Chapters 1, 2, 4, then Part IV (chapters 13 to
22), then 23. You will meet the machinery only in one-line verdicts.

**The Sanskrit path.** Chapters 1 to 4, then Part IV entire, then 12
(how polysemy became data), then 23 and 25. Appendix B supports the
script and pronunciation.

**The engineering path.** Chapters 1, 5 to 11, then 13 (to see the
system at work on real verses), then 23 and 24. Appendix A reproduces
everything.

**The linguistics path.** Chapters 1, 3, 4, 8, then 17 and 18 (sphoṭa,
phoneme, word, sentence), then 24.

**The cover-to-cover path.** All of it, in order. The book was composed
this way, and the seams are built to carry you across.

## Conventions {.unnumbered}

Boxed passages marked *For the engineer*, *For the paṇḍit*, and *In the
vṛtti* go deeper for one door at a time; skipping them never breaks the
plot. Each chapter ends with a short summary called *The thread so far*.
Claims about the verification system carry receipts, short pointers to
the public repository files or the companion paper, so nothing asks to
be taken on trust. Each verse entry in Part IV gives the Devanagari, the
transliteration, the translation, the complete commentary, and the
machine's verdicts.
