# About the Author {.unnumbered}

Dr Sharath Sathish is a data scientist and innovation leader based in
London, with more than two decades of engineering practice spanning
aerospace, oil and gas, and energy. His career began with inertial
navigation: developing gyroscopes and accelerometers for launch vehicles
and satellites, work that included resolving a years-long technical
impasse in gyroscope development that contributed to India's landmark
interplanetary missions. From there the path ran through diagnostic and
prognostic modelling of gas turbines at GE, a doctorate on supercritical
carbon dioxide power cycles for renewable energy and industrial waste
heat recovery, a postdoctoral fellowship on supercritical CO2 turbomachinery, and his
present role leading global pricing analytics, where he applies machine
learning, game theory, and causal inference at industrial scale. His
published research runs from turbomachinery design and thermodynamic
cycle optimization (in venues including the *Journal of Supercritical
Fluids*, *Applied Thermal Engineering*, and the ASME Turbo Expo series,
along with a patent on waste heat recovery cycles) to recent work in
mechanistic interpretability and AI safety, including Active Circuit
Discovery (*Symmetry*, 2026) and the Prabodha and Pramana research
programmes.

The Sanskrit in this book has a longer history than the theorems. As a
schoolboy at Sri Ramakrishna Vidyashala, Mysuru, he was introduced to
Vedānta; as an engineering undergraduate in Bengaluru (1999 to 2003) he
studied Sanskrit through Akṣaram, Samskrita Bharati's centre, completing
four levels of its correspondence course while completing a mechanical
engineering degree. The two educations ran in parallel for twenty-five
years, through space technology, energy systems, machine learning, and
latterly neuroscience and AI, each stage adding a new lens: the
engineer's respect for systems that must not fail, the scientist's
demand for evidence, and the śāstric tradition's conviction that
language, precisely used, is an instrument of truth.

This book sits at the meeting point of those lenses, and inside a larger
mission: infusing value and science simultaneously into AI development
by blending engineering, neuroscience, and darśana paradigms into the
skeleton of generative AI, so that systems that produce language can be
held, mechanically, to what is true. His work in mechanistic
interpretability and AI safety pursues the same goal from the other
side: understanding what these models actually compute, and gating what
they produce.
